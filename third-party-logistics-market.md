<p><p>Third-party logistics (3PL) can be categorized into three market types: B2B, B2C, and C2C. In B2B, logistics providers assist businesses with transporting goods between companies, enhancing supply chain efficiency. B2C focuses on delivering products directly to consumers, appealing to retail and e-commerce sectors. Lastly, C2C involves logistics services facilitating transactions between consumers, often through online platforms. Each type leverages 3PL to streamline operations, improve delivery times, and reduce costs, tailored to the specific needs of the market.</p></p>
