<p><p>Asphalt shingles are a popular roofing material featuring two primary types: organic and fiberglass. Organic asphalt shingles contain a base made from recycled paper or felt, giving them flexibility and durability. However, they are heavier and can be less resistant to moisture. In contrast, fiberglass asphalt shingles incorporate a fiberglass mat for reinforcement, making them lighter, more fire-resistant, and often more energy-efficient. Both types offer various styles and colors, catering to diverse aesthetic preferences and functional needs in roofing solutions.</p></p>
