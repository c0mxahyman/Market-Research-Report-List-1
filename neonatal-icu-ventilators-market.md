<p><p>The neonatal ICU ventilators market comprises two main types: invasive and non-invasive ventilators. Invasive ventilators are used for critically ill neonates who require endotracheal intubation, providing controlled respiratory support through direct airway access. Non-invasive ventilators, on the other hand, deliver respiratory support via masks or nasal interfaces, reducing the risk of trauma and infection. Both types are crucial in managing respiratory distress syndromes in premature and critically ill infants, catering to varying clinical needs in neonatal intensive care settings.</p></p>
