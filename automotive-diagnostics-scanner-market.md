<p><p>The automotive diagnostics scanner market is segmented into corded and cordless (Bluetooth) types. Corded scanners are directly connected to the vehicle's diagnostic port, offering reliable and uninterrupted communication. They are typically preferred for their consistent power supply and robust data handling. In contrast, cordless scanners utilize Bluetooth technology to connect wirelessly, providing greater mobility and ease of use. This flexibility allows technicians to move around the vehicle freely while diagnosing issues, enhancing overall efficiency in automotive repair and maintenance.</p></p>
